The History of pickles:

Pickled Cucumbers, or as they are more commenly known as, pickles, have a suprisngly long and vast history.
Pickles are thought to be more then 4,000 years old! Some of the earliest known examples of pickles dates back to 2400 B.C.
when mesopotamians pickleed cucumbers for later consumption. It is even known that Cleopatra and Julius Caesar both ate pickles
since the first believed it improved her beauty, and the latter believed that it made you a more powerful warrior.
During the age of exploration, the popularity of pickles really took off. On his voyage to the Americas, Christopher Columbus
brought pickles with him on his trip, since they stayed good for a long period of time and prevented scurvy. During the 19th and 20th
centuries H.J. Heinz would start one of the successful pickling companies ever.

The making of a pickle:

Pickled cucumbers are made as their name suggests, pickling. A cucmber is submerged in a solution of acidic brine which ferments them
and makes them go sour and salty. The proccess is started by preparing the brine which is a mix of spices, which traditionally includes
garlic, horseradish, and dill. The most impostant ingrediant in the brin though is salt, lots and lots of salt. The more salt that you
add the more sour the pickle will turn out to be. The sourness of the pcikle is also influenced by the amount of time given to the
fermentation proccess of the pickles.

The diffrent types of pickles:

There are a plethera of diffrent types of pickles. Types of pickles include: Bread-and-butter, Gherkin, Kosher dill, Hungarian,
Polish and German, and Lime. All these pickles differ mainly in the type of cucumber used and the ingredients used in the brine.
For example pickles such as the Bread-and-butter, and Polish are sweeter in comparison to the other types of pickles since
their brine includes sugar. Pickles such as Gherkins are made with baby cucumbers while most other pickles are made with
full-size cucumbers.
